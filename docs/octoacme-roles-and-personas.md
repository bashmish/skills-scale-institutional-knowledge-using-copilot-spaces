# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers orchestrate the end-to-end release process, ensuring all pre-release requirements are met before deployment. They coordinate between development and QA teams and communicate release schedules and status to stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and manage release schedules and milestones
- Verify pre-release requirements and acceptance criteria are satisfied
- Coordinate deployment activities across dev and QA teams
- Communicate release status and timelines to stakeholders
- Maintain release notes and deployment documentation

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, on-schedule releases to production
- Minimize deployment risk through thorough pre-release verification
- Keep all stakeholders informed and aligned on release status

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews with development and QA
- Stakeholder release announcements and status updates
- Go/no-go decision meetings prior to deployment

### Interacts With
- **Developers** — to confirm feature completeness and resolve last-minute issues
- **QA Lead** — to verify testing is complete and quality gates are passed
- **Project Manager** — to align on release schedule and risk mitigation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the quality strategy and test planning for the project. They oversee end-to-end, manual, and regression testing cycles and serve as the final authority on production readiness.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the overall test strategy and quality standards
- Plan and oversee manual, automated, and regression testing cycles
- Validate acceptance criteria are met before release
- Track and report defects, test coverage, and quality metrics
- Verify production readiness and participate in go/no-go decisions

### Goals
- Ensure every release meets quality and reliability standards
- Reduce defect escape rate to production
- Provide clear, data-driven quality signals to the team

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews with developers and release manager
- Daily defect triage and test status updates
- Quality reports and production-readiness sign-offs

### Interacts With
- **Developers** — to clarify requirements, reproduce defects, and validate fixes
- **Release Manager** — to confirm quality gates are met before deployment
- **Project Manager** — to report quality status and flag risks to the schedule

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts translate business needs into clear technical requirements and user stories. They align acceptance criteria across stakeholders and validate that technical delivery meets business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Write user stories and detailed acceptance criteria
- Facilitate requirements workshops and grooming sessions
- Validate delivered features against business objectives
- Manage requirement changes and communicate impacts to the team

### Goals
- Ensure development efforts are tightly aligned with business value
- Reduce rework caused by ambiguous or missing requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between business stakeholders and engineering

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and backlog grooming with the team
- Acceptance criteria documentation and user story refinement
- Stakeholder sign-off sessions on delivered functionality

### Interacts With
- **Product Manager** — to translate the product vision into actionable requirements
- **Project Manager** — to surface requirement risks and schedule dependencies
- **Developers** — to clarify requirements and validate implementation details

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user flows, UI designs, and interactive prototypes. They gather user feedback, ensure design consistency across features, and champion the end-user perspective throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and synthesize findings into design decisions
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity UI designs
- Define and maintain interaction patterns and design systems
- Collaborate with developers to ensure accurate implementation of designs
- Validate delivered UI against design specifications and usability standards

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, consistent, and accessible user experiences
- Reduce usability issues discovered late in the development cycle
- Ensure design decisions are grounded in user research and feedback

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and prototype walkthroughs with product and engineering
- Usability testing sessions and synthesis readouts
- Design handoff documentation and component specifications

### Interacts With
- **Product Manager** — to align design direction with product vision and priorities
- **Developers** — to collaborate on implementation feasibility and design accuracy
- **QA Lead** — to define visual and interaction acceptance criteria

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
Support Leads coordinate post-release customer support, triage incoming user issues, and collect feedback to inform future improvements. They serve as the primary voice of the customer within the project team.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate customer support operations following each release
- Triage and prioritize inbound user issues and bug reports
- Collect and synthesize customer feedback for the product roadmap
- Escalate critical issues to the appropriate development or product contacts
- Advocate for the customer perspective in planning and retrospective discussions

### Goals
- Ensure customers receive timely, effective support after each release
- Surface recurring issues and feedback trends to drive product improvements
- Reduce time-to-resolution for customer-reported issues

### Typical Communication
- Post-release support handoff briefings with the team
- Issue triage reports and escalation notifications
- Feedback summaries shared with product and project leadership

### Interacts With
- **Developers** — to escalate and resolve customer-reported bugs
- **Product Manager** — to share customer feedback and influence roadmap priorities
- **Project Manager** — to report support status and flag issues impacting upcoming releases
